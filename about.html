About

Im the Head of Instruction for the Institute for Advanced Analytics at Bellarmine University, where I teach a variety of analytics topics to graduate students and coordinate a team of specialist instructors.

I completed my Ph.D.in the department of Psychological and Brain Sciences at the University of Louisville, under the direction of Cara Cashon. I studied cognitive and developmental sciences, and designed research to understand the development of face processing and causal cognition in infancy.

After completing my Ph.D., I worked as an associate research scientist, providing analytics support for several Healthcare, Social Science, and Education research projects at the Pacific Institute for Research and Evaluation.

I am also a home renovation hack, wannabe bourbon aficionado, actual beer snob, amatuer weather junkie, and avid dog parent.
